Dear Sir/Madam,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript entitled "Security benchmarking of AI-generated web application code: An OWASP-based comparative study of coding LLMs" for consideration as a full length research article in the World Journal of Advanced Engineering Technology and Sciences.

This manuscript presents a comparative empirical study of the security posture of web application code generated by four contemporary AI coding systems: Gemini 3.5 Flash, DeepSeek V4 Flash, Kimi K2.5, and GPT-5.4-mini. The study uses 10 standardized web application prompts, static application security testing with Semgrep OSS, manual review, and OWASP Top 10 mapping to evaluate 40 generated code samples. The findings show recurring security-by-default gaps, especially missing CSRF protection, insecure cookie/session settings, path traversal risks, and security misconfigurations.

We believe the manuscript is suitable for WJAETS because it addresses a current engineering and technological challenge at the intersection of artificial intelligence, software engineering, and cybersecurity. The work is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration by any other journal.

All authors have reviewed and approved the manuscript for submission. There are no conflicts of interest to declare.
